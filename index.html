Helpline Volunteer applications are open!

Helpline Volunteers provide confidential and identity-affirming emotional support and resource referrals, taking 3.5-hour shifts weekly between Fridays-Mondays, 1:00PM-7:00PM. Applicants for the Fall Training Class must be available on Saturdays 12:30-5:30PM from September 26, 2020 to Dec. 19, 2020 for the 48-hour peer counselor training. Applicants will go through two interview rounds and must be at least 18 years old before starting to take shifts. Read about the Helpline Training Curriculum.

Applications for the Fall Training Class close on August 28 and recur twice every year.

Black Lives Matter.

SQSH stands in solidarity against systemic violence against the Black community. We train our Helpline volunteers protest-specific peer counseling skills and require a 4-hour anti-racism and anti-bias training.

National ResourcesThe Bail ProjectArch City DefendersAction St. LouisMissouri Black Protestor Relief Fund


SQSH is working towards racial equity and justice.

Currently, we are:
  • Training our Helpline Volunteers in protest-specific peer counseling skills in order to offer SQSH as a resource to support the Black Lives Matter Movement and our local protest community.
  • Utilizing our outreach platforms to boost bail funds, protests, and other actions in support of BLM.
  • Mobilizing our members to attend actions and community events in support of BLM.
  • Engaging in conversations internally and with our community partners to assess how we can most effectively support the work of local Black-led organizations.
  • Building and maintaining a reading list of educational resources about race that we continually use to improve our members’ level of anti-racism education.
  • Beginning every monthly meeting with a moment of silence to acknowledge the deep injustice, pain and heaviness in the murders of George Floyd, Tony McDade, Nina Pop, Breonna Taylor, Ahmaud Arbery, Kiwi Herring, Michael Brown, and countless other Black individuals.
  • Continuing to require and implement a 4-hour anti-racism training for our Helpline Volunteers as part of their Foundational Training when they join SQSH.
  • Developing an in-house, multi-session anti-bias training specifically tailored to various teams in SQSH to interrupt the impact of bias on every aspect of SQSH’s operations.
  • Engaging in AROC’s Learning to Action Circles for our White and mixed-race volunteers to unlearn and disrupt their relationship to White privilege and hold each other accountable to ending White supremacy in their lives.
In the long-term, we hope to:
  • Form a cross-team anti-racism working group within SQSH to research and analyze which in which we can combat racism within SQSH and as an organization. This includes a systematic study of why SQSH is lacking in racial diversity and channeling resources towards addressing these reasons.
  • Provide all new members in SQSH with required anti-racism education alongside peer counseling training.

What We Do

We offer a free, confidential, identity-affirming, and volunteer-run helpline serving the St. Louis region. Our trained peer counselors provide compassionate support to any LGBTQIA+ community members, allies, and service providers.

Who We Are

Our mission is to serve and strengthen the St. Louis LGBTQIA+ community—by providing an empathetic listening ear, by connecting callers safely and reliably to LGBTQIA-affirming resources, and by increasing the visibility of LGBTQIA+ concerns, needs, and resources in the St. Louis region.
