Support SQSH

Donations make our work possible.

Since its founding, SQSH has answered approximately 168 calls and has trained 39 Helpline Volunteers. Our generous supporters allow us to run our services completely free. As a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization, we need your support to continue the valuable community-building and mutual aid work that we do. Any gifts made to SQSH will be directly invested in supporting the St. Louis queer community. If you value the work we do, please consider a donation to keep SQSH going. Thank you!

Help SQSH Thrive Alongside Queer Community Across Missouri & Illinois!

We need your help to sustain SQSH's lifesaving services as we expand to provide LGBTQIA-affirming peer support across Missouri and Illinois. Your financial support today will allow us to restore our financial health and continue our work improving queer lives across the midwest.

FAQs for Potential Donors

How is SQSH funded?
We started this project in 2019 using an initial $5,000 Civic Scholars grant from Washington University's Gephardt Institute for Civic & Community Engagement. In our first year, we were sustained by the following major funding streams: a $2,500 Mini Grant from the St. Louis Area Violence Prevention Commission, $1,900 from our Facebook Fundraiser, and $400 from our 2019 Benefit Show by C U Next Tuesday.

Moving forward, we hope to sustain this project through local donations, fundraisers, grants, and awards. We spend our funds on training, rent, technology, volunteer support, insurance, and PR. We aim to publish annual financial reports to be fully transparent with how we obtain funding and spend our money.
How will SQSH spend my donation money?
We use the majority of our donations to operate our Helpline, including to pay for office space, phone lines, and insurance. We also use donations to provide training to our Helpline Volunteers, buy call-taking equipment, financially support low-income volunteers, and fund outreach projects.
Will my donation be tax deductible?
Yes! SQSH received our official 501(c)(3) non-profit status on June 9, 2020. You can view our 501(c)(3) approval letter from the IRS here.
